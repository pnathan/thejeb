# Pierre-Simon Laplace, Traite de mecanique celeste (Mechanique Celeste), Book IV (1799)
# Curated representative passage (public domain work). Provenance: refs.bib laplace1799mecanique.

The tides are the oscillations of the sea produced by the gravitational attractions of the sun and the
moon. But the sea does not assume at every instant the figure of equilibrium; the waters, being fluid
and possessed of inertia, respond to the periodic forces of the two bodies as a dynamical system of
forced oscillations, governed by the equations of the motion of fluids. It is therefore not enough to
compute the equilibrium tide; one must integrate the differential equations of the moving ocean under
the disturbing forces, which vary with the positions of the moon and sun. The cause is the gravitation
of these bodies; the true theory is the dynamical one, and from it the observed heights and times at
each port may be deduced by calculation.
