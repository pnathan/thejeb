# Galileo Galilei, Dialogue Concerning the Two Chief World Systems, Fourth Day (1632)
# Curated representative passage (public domain work; trans. after Drake). Provenance: refs.bib galileo1632dialogue.

The ebbing and flowing of the seas cannot be produced except by the motion of their basins. Now the
Earth carries the basins of the seas, and the Earth has two motions: the diurnal rotation upon its
axis and the annual revolution about the sun. These two motions compound so that any part of the
Earth's surface is now accelerated and now retarded in its passage through space. This continual
alteration of speed makes the contained waters run now toward one end of their basins, now toward the
other, and so they rise and fall. I hold it therefore as certain that the tides follow from the
Earth's motion, and that a motionless Earth could show no tides at all. As for those who refer the
cause to the moon, attributing to her some dominion over the water by an occult property, I will not
give ear to such notions, for it is the compounding of the two terrestrial motions, twice a day, that
produces the effect, the moon having nothing to do with it.
